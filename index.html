About Me

For most of the last decade I've worked as a project manager at a market research firm, where uncovering and analyzing large collections of data was an essential part of my role. This gradually led me to an interest in automation and business intelligence, both of which started me down the path of programming.

What started off as building simple terminal games in Python eventually grew into an interest in web development, where I started to experiment with building some (at the time, very ugly) websites and first wrapping my head around JavaScript and JQuery. Since then, I've gone on to learn React, TypeScript, Node, SQL, Docker, GraphQL, and recently Golang.

I enjoy exploring the React ecosystem as well as improving my backend skills by building new GraphQL servers. I'm excited by all the advancements that have swept through web development over the last few years and hope I can contribute to a small part of it.

When I'm not fighting the computer, I enjoy spending time with my young boys, gaming, and reading Japanese novels.
